Scope

This Policy applies to information collected through our website, email, and phone. It does not apply to third-party websites linked from our site (including retailers, apps, or Google Maps), which have their own policies.

Information We Collect

We collect the categories of information below. We do not knowingly collect Social Security numbers, government ID numbers, precise geolocation for tracking, or health records through this site.

  • Identifiers — name, email address, phone number if you call us, and online identifiers such as IP address or cookie IDs
  • Customer records — information you submit in forms (e.g., message content, program interest)
  • Internet or network activity — pages viewed, referring URL, browser type, device type, and interaction with our site
  • Inferences — limited preferences derived from your use of the site (e.g., sections visited), only where permitted by law and your cookie choices

Sources: directly from you; automatically from your device and browser; and from service providers that help us operate the site.

We do not intentionally collect “sensitive personal information” as defined under California law (such as health data or precise geolocation) through our forms.

How We Use Personal Information

We use personal information for business and commercial purposes including:

  • Providing requested materials, responding to messages, and delivering customer support
  • Operating, maintaining, securing, and debugging the website
  • Analytics and performance measurement when you accept Analytics cookies
  • Measuring affiliate link performance when you accept Marketing cookies
  • Complying with law, responding to legal requests, and enforcing our Terms
  • Creating aggregated or de-identified data that cannot reasonably identify you

We do not use personal information for automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects.

How We Disclose Personal Information

We may disclose personal information to:

  • Service providers — hosting, email delivery, analytics, security, and IT vendors under contractual confidentiality and security obligations
  • Affiliate / advertising partners — only when you accept Marketing cookies or click affiliate links, subject to their policies
  • Legal and safety — when required by law, subpoena, or to protect rights, safety, and security
  • Business transfers — in connection with a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, with notice where required

Sale or Sharing of Personal Information

We do not sell your personal information for money. Some U.S. state laws define “sale” or “sharing” broadly to include certain advertising or analytics disclosures to third parties. Where those laws apply, you may opt out as described below. We do not sell or share the personal information of consumers we know are under 16 without affirmative authorization.

How to opt out: use Reject or Cookie Settings in our cookie banner to disable Analytics and Marketing cookies; email hello@yogahome.com with subject line “Do Not Sell or Share”; or call +1 (918) 520-0552. We honor opt-out preference signals such as Global Privacy Control (GPC) where required by law.

Retention

  • Contact and inquiry records: up to 24 months after last interaction, unless a longer period is required by law or you request deletion sooner
  • Email marketing: until you unsubscribe, opt out, or request deletion
  • Server and security logs: typically up to 12 months
  • Cookie consent records: up to 12 months

We delete or de-identify information when it is no longer needed for the purposes described above.

Your U.S. State Privacy Rights

Depending on your state of residence, you may have some or all of the following rights, subject to legal exceptions:

  • Right to know / access — confirm whether we process your personal information and obtain a copy
  • Right to delete — request deletion of personal information we collected from you
  • Right to correct — request correction of inaccurate personal information
  • Right to opt out — of sale, sharing, or targeted advertising as defined by applicable state law
  • Right to limit — use and disclosure of sensitive personal information (we do not use sensitive information for purposes requiring opt-out beyond what is permitted by law)
  • Right to portability — receive certain data in a portable format where required
  • Right to non-discrimination — we will not deny goods or services, charge different prices, or provide a different level of service solely because you exercised privacy rights
  • Right to appeal — if we deny your request, certain states (e.g., Virginia, Colorado) allow you to appeal our decision

California Residents (CCPA / CPRA)

California residents may request disclosure of categories and specific pieces of personal information collected, sources, purposes, and third parties to whom we disclose information. You may request deletion and correction, opt out of sale/sharing, and limit use of sensitive personal information. We will verify your request using information you provide. Authorized agents may submit requests with proof of authorization. To submit a request, email hello@yogahome.com or write to our Tulsa address. We aim to respond within 45 days as required by California law.

Under California Civil Code § 1798.83, California residents may request information about disclosure of personal information to third parties for their direct marketing purposes. We do not share personal information with unaffiliated third parties for their direct marketing purposes as defined in that statute.

Other State Laws

Residents of Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Utah, Texas, Oregon, Montana, and other states with consumer privacy laws may exercise similar rights by contacting us. Oklahoma consumers may have rights under applicable Oklahoma privacy statutes as they take effect and apply to our operations. If we deny a request where an appeal is available, we will explain how to appeal in our response.

How to Exercise Your Rights

Email and Communications (CAN-SPAM)

If you receive marketing emails from us, they will identify Yoga at Home as the sender and include a working unsubscribe link or instructions. We process opt-out requests within 10 business days as required by the CAN-SPAM Act. Transactional messages related to your requests may still be sent.

Telephone (TCPA)

If you provide a phone number, we may call or text you only as permitted by law and any consent you provide. You may revoke consent by emailing us or requesting removal during a call.

Children’s Privacy (COPPA)

Our website is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 in violation of the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). If you believe a child under 13 provided information, contact us and we will delete it promptly.

Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to the information we process, including HTTPS encryption in transit. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

Do Not Track

Some browsers offer “Do Not Track” signals. Because there is no uniform industry standard, we do not respond to all DNT signals. You may use our cookie banner and state opt-out rights instead.

International Visitors

We are based in the United States. If you access the site from outside the U.S., your information may be processed in the U.S. Visitors from the EEA, UK, or Switzerland may have additional rights under GDPR, including legal bases of consent, contract, legitimate interests, and legal obligation; the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority; and the right to withdraw consent. Contact us to exercise those rights.
